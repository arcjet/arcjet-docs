Users paste sensitive data into AI prompts - credit card numbers, phone numbers,
passwords, and other PII - often without realizing the risk. Once that data
reaches your AI provider it may appear in logs, training pipelines, or model
outputs.

Arcjet sensitive info detection scans prompt content inside your application
before it reaches the AI provider. Detected PII is blocked before it leaves your
app environment.

## Configuring sensitive info detection

`deny: ["CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER", "EMAIL"]` (Python: `deny=[SensitiveInfoEntityType.CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER, SensitiveInfoEntityType.EMAIL]`) -
the list of PII entity types to block. Remove any types your application
legitimately handles - for example, a support bot that collects phone numbers
should remove `"PHONE_NUMBER"` from the deny list.

Built-in entity types: `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `PHONE_NUMBER`, `EMAIL`,
`IP_ADDRESS`. The JS SDK additionally supports `URL`. See the [full
reference](/sensitive-info/reference) for the complete list and for defining
your own custom detectors.

`sensitiveInfoValue` (JS) / `sensitive_info_value` (Python) - The text to scan.
Pass the user's prompt or the most recent message. You can also pass the full
conversation history if you want to scan all messages, not just the latest one.

`mode: "DRY_RUN"` (JS) / `mode=Mode.DRY_RUN` (Python) - Logs detections without
blocking. Use this to audit what PII appears in prompts before switching to
`"LIVE"`.

## Combine with abuse protection

Sensitive info detection controls *what* reaches your AI provider. To also
block hostile instructions and jailbreak attempts, combine it with
[prompt injection detection](/ai-protection/prompt-injection). To block
automated clients and enforce per-user budgets, combine it with
[AI abuse protection](/ai-protection/abuse-protection) and
[AI budget control](/ai-protection/budget-control). For agentic pipelines
that don't route through HTTP, [Arcjet Guards](/guards) provides the same
PII detection directly inside tool handlers.
