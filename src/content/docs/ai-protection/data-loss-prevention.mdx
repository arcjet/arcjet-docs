Users paste sensitive data into AI prompts - credit card numbers, phone numbers,
passwords, and other PII - often without realizing the risk. Once that data
reaches your AI provider it may appear in logs, training pipelines, or model
outputs.

Arcjet sensitive info detection scans prompt content inside your application
before it reaches the AI provider. Detected PII is blocked before it leaves your
app environment.

## Configuring sensitive info detection

`deny: ["CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER", "EMAIL"]` - the list of PII entity types to
block. Remove any types your application legitimately handles - for example, a
support bot that collects phone numbers should remove `"PHONE_NUMBER"` from the
deny list.

Available entity types include: `CREDIT_CARD_NUMBER`, `PHONE_NUMBER`, `EMAIL`,
`IP_ADDRESS`, `URL`. See the [full reference](/sensitive-info/reference) for the
complete list.

`sensitiveInfoValue` - The text to scan. Pass the user's prompt or the most
recent message. You can also pass the full conversation history if you want to
scan all messages, not just the latest one.

`mode: "DRY_RUN"` - Logs detections without blocking. Use this to audit what PII
appears in prompts before switching to `"LIVE"`.

## Combine with abuse protection

Sensitive info detection controls *what* reaches your AI provider. To also
block hostile instructions and jailbreak attempts, combine it with
[prompt injection detection](/ai-protection/prompt-injection). To block
automated clients and enforce per-user budgets, combine it with
[AI abuse protection](/ai-protection/abuse-protection) and
[AI budget control](/ai-protection/budget-control).
